# Motionary — Design Context

A reference library of motion patterns (transitions, hover effects, scroll
animations, micro-interactions) for developers. Each entry pairs a live demo
with a copy-ready AI prompt that can be pasted into a coding agent.

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary**: Developers building modern web UIs — frontend engineers, design
engineers, indie hackers — many of whom drive AI coding agents (Claude, Cursor,
v0) to scaffold features. They arrive looking for a specific motion pattern,
want to see it move, and want to grab the prompt and go.

**Context of use**: Mid-task, not exploratory browsing. They have a feature in
mind and need a fast, accurate motion reference. Time-on-page is short. Bounce
to "copy prompt → back to their editor" should feel friction-free.

**Job-to-be-done**:
1. Browse a category, scan motion in real time, recognize what they need
2. Open the example, watch it loop, confirm it's right
3. Copy the prompt and leave

### Brand Personality

**Three words**: Technical · Premium · Modern.

**Voice**: Developer-direct. Terse, accurate, reads like good docs. No
cheerleading, no exclamation marks, no emoji. Labels are functional ("Copy
prompt", "18 examples", "Replay"), never cute.

**Emotional target**: Confidence. Users should feel the site is made by people
who care about craft — that the prompts and demos are reliable, the motion is
done right, and the surface itself respects their time.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme**: Dark mode is the default and the brand's "natural" surface. Light
mode is offered as a toggle, designed with equal care, but the marketing/hero
moments are tuned for dark.

**Reference (good)**: motion.dev — clean structure, restraint, motion-first
hierarchy. The user-supplied AI-business mockup contributes its **color
language** (deep violet/purple on near-black, soft glow on accent CTAs,
pill-shaped buttons and badges, layered card composition with depth) and its
**layout DNA** (a large central focal subject with supporting info ranged
around it, stats and trust strip below).

**Anti-references**:

- Heavy 3D chrome / liquid-metal / generic AI-orb hero art
- Cluttered marketing screenshots stacked at angles to fill space
- "AI startup landing page" template energy — overpromised, underbuilt
- Material Design density and heavy elevation
- Generic Tailwind starter (slate-900 + indigo-500 + zero personality)
- Corporate dashboard chrome (status pills, filter bars, breadcrumbs everywhere)

**Color palette intent**:

| Role | Dark | Light |
|---|---|---|
| Background | near-black (`#0a0a0b`) | white (`#ffffff`) |
| Surface (raised) | `#18181b` | `#f4f4f5` |
| Border (subtle) | `#27272a` | `#e4e4e7` |
| Foreground | `#fafafa` | `#0a0a0b` |
| Muted text | `#a1a1aa` | `#52525b` |
| Accent (primary) | violet `#a78bfa` | violet `#7c3aed` |
| Accent (vivid CTA) | violet `#8b5cf6` with subtle glow | violet `#7c3aed` |

The accent appears sparingly: CTAs, focused links, the demo "playing" indicator,
selection highlight. Surfaces stay quiet so motion is the loudest thing on the
page.

**Typography**: Geist Sans for UI, Geist Mono for prompt blocks and counts
(numbers in stats). Sizes follow a generous, almost editorial scale on the
hero/category headers; tighten for cards. No mid-weight overload — favor a
clear contrast between display weight and body.

**Surface treatments**:

- Soft 1px borders, never harsh
- Rounded corners: `rounded-2xl` for cards, `rounded-full` for buttons/pills
- Light noise/grid texture on demo containers (so the motion has something to
  read against), not on chrome
- Glow on accent only, faint and short-radius (no neon halos)

**Motion of the chrome itself** (the site, not the demos):

- Transitions are quick (120–220ms), eased (`cubic-bezier(0.32, 0.72, 0, 1)`)
- Hover states are felt, not seen — small scale or color shift, never bouncy
- Page transitions use crossfade or short slide, ~250ms
- Demos auto-play in view via IntersectionObserver, pause off-screen
- `prefers-reduced-motion` collapses non-essential motion to instant; demo
  cards show a static first-frame instead of looping

### Design Principles

1. **Motion is the message.** The site itself is a demo of the craft. Every
   surface — nav, cards, copy buttons — should move with intent. If a
   transition feels generic, it's wrong here.

2. **Premium restraint.** Quiet surfaces, loud motion. The accent color, glow,
   and decorative noise are seasoning, not the meal. When in doubt, remove a
   layer.

3. **Developer-direct copy.** Labels are short and accurate. No marketing
   adjectives, no emoji, no exclamation. Microcopy reads like a well-written
   API doc.

4. **One focus per view.** Home: the category grid. Category page: the example
   gallery. Detail page: the demo, then the prompt. Don't compete for attention
   with secondary CTAs, banners, or recommendation strips above the fold.

5. **Accessible by default.** `prefers-reduced-motion` is honored on day one.
   Contrast meets WCAG AA in both themes. Every interactive element is
   keyboard-reachable; the copy button announces its state to screen readers.
